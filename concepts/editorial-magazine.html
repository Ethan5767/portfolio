The Lead

He builds models in one language,
writes scholarships in another,
and answers his mother in a third.

Kimtong Peng works at the seam where mechanical engineering ends and machine intelligence begins. Educated at Sungkyunkwan University in Seoul, raised in Phnom Penh, and now leading research and development for NeWwave from a remote desk in Cambodia, he is the rare profile that refuses a single column — engineer, organizer, student of three cultures, and, in his off-hours, the patient Vice President of a small but stubborn association of Cambodian students living in Korea.

He arrived in Korea in 2022 on a GKS-U scholarship — a fully funded berth from the Republic of Korea's National Institute for International Education — and spent his first year almost entirely inside Daegu University's Korean language program. By March 2023 he had a Mechanical Engineering degree from Sungkyunkwan; by January 2024 he was leading AI research at NeWwave.

To bridge anything — disciplines, languages, countries — you first have to be willing to stand in the middle of it and be misread for a while.
— A working motto, in three languages
On the work

At NeWwave, his title — AI Research & Development Lead — sits where his two top-listed skills meet on the page: Artificial Intelligence and Research and Development. Two years and five months in, and counting.

On the side

Since July 2023 he has served the Cambodian Student Association in Korea — first as Secretary in social services, then, from July 2024, as Vice President on the education portfolio. CSAK is a small non-governmental volunteer body: it promotes Cambodia's image in Korea and looks after Cambodian students who, like Kimtong once did, arrive in a country whose alphabet they cannot yet read.

On the prize

In November 2023, between the end of his degree and the beginning of his AI work, he took the Grand Prize at the 2nd Asia Youth Future Forum — a joint convening of the Asia Exchange Association and Pyeongtech International Exchange Foundation — for proposals on climate strategy and industrial transition. He was, that same autumn, a semi-finalist at the Climate Science Olympiad. He had been a semi-finalist there once before, in 2021, when he was still a high-school student in Phnom Penh.

Beltei International School, Phnom Penh, 2009–2021. Twelve years in one set of corridors.
